                                <p>aol sux
big providers like yours tend to have many abusive users, many servers &amp; channels are forced to ban everybody from there. Although it seems unfair, it&#8217;s unfortunately necessary and there&#8217;s no way around the bans. </p>

<p>aol sux 2
you can either put up with it, or switch to a smaller local provider for better service at the same cost without the bad reputation</p>

<p>banned! help?
type /names #channelname to find ops (nicks like @foo).  Then /msg foo please let me in. If /names gives no response or shows no ops, or if they ignore you, you&#8217;re out of luck, just go elsewhere.</p>

<p>bot help? no!
Sorry, bot help is beyond the scope of this channel. Instead, try #egghelp or use /list and look for &#8220;egg&#8221; or &#8220;bot&#8221; channels. NOTE: many EFnet servers do NOT allow bots at all, so first use /motd to check each server&#8217;s policies.</p>

<p>bot = ?
a (ro)bot is an automated IRC program, sometimes used to maintain channels or give info. Bots are not allowed on many servers. Type /motd to read your server&#8217;s rules on bots and other issues.</p>

<p>ctcp
CTCP (client to client protocol) gives info about someone&#8217;s client &#8212;> /ctcp $N PING  tells you how long it takes for your /msg to reach me. Try replacing PING with TIME, VERSION or USERINFO too.</p>

<p>dcc
DCC stands for Direct Client Communication, lets you transfer files or chat without lag or splits.</p>

<p>dcc ignore
For your own protection against viruses and trojan horse attacks, mirc by default ignores all but a few &#8216;safe&#8217; file types. You can change those protection settings at your own risk, see http://irchelp.org/warez for more info</p>

<p>file exchange
IRC is primarily for chat. Some people exchange files, but it is illegal, somewhat difficult, and you risk getting viruses or &#8216;hacked&#8217;. You should first chat and learn how IRC works. For more info, see http://irchelp.org/warez</p>

<p>invisible
invisible mode gives you some privacy, others won&#8217;t find your nick by /names or /who from outside a channel or with wildcards, unless they are on the same channel as you already or know your nick exactly.</p>

<p>IRCNAME
First I need to know your shell. Type this now (note capitalization!) and wait for more instructions: /exec -o echo $$SHELL</p>

<p>For sh/ksh/bash:  /quit from IRC and type this at shell prompt &#8212;> export IRCNAME=&#8221;whatever&#8221; &lt;&#8212; (note caps and quotes!) To make the change permanent, use a UNIX text editor to put that into your .profile file.</p>

<p>For (t)csh:  /quit from IRC and type this at shell prompt &#8212;>  setenv IRCNAME &#8220;whatever&#8221; &lt;&#8212; (note caps and quotes) To make the change permanent, use a UNIX text editor to put that into your .cshrc file.</p>

<p>For vax/vms: /quit from IRC and type this at shell prompt &#8212;> define IRCNAME &#8220;John Dork&#8221; &lt;&#8212; (note caps and quotes). To make the change permanent, use a text editor to put that into your login.com file.</p>

<p>irc op?
IRC operators are everywhere including here, but they don&#8217;t usually interfere in user/channel affairs like takeovers or restoring ops. Why did you want an IRC op?</p>

<p>ircii /list
To list channels page by page, type /set hold<em>mode on. Then /list -min 5 (for channels with at least 5 people) and /flush when you&#8217;ve seen enough. Finally /set hold</em>mode off.</p>

<p>list disconnect
your current server is too impatient to send you the huge channel list, try other servers or see the searchable channel list at http://www.irchelp.org/chanlist</p>

<p>logging abuse
see http://www.irchelp.org/irchelp/misc/irclog.html for how to report abuse to the appropriate IRC operators.</p>

<p>mirc
if /help or the FAQ doesn&#8217;t answer your question, try #mirchelp or #mirc</p>

<p>mirc dcc send
do this: (1) In File|Options|Connect|LocalInfo, delete all text in Local Host and IP Address (2) Check always get LOCAL HOST, lookup method SERVER (3) Disconnect from the server and reconnect</p>

<p>mode +tn
+tn turns on 2 common channel modes, <t> means only ops can change the topic and <n> means no messages from outside the channel.</p>

<p>modes for channels
Channel modes: <t> only ops set topic, <n> no msg&#8217;s from outside channel, &lt;p/s> private/secret, <k> key/passworded, <i> invite-only, <m> moderated, only ops speak, <v> voice given in moderated channel, <l> limits # people.</p>

<p>msg me not
pls ask on the channel so others can help if I&#8217;m busy/clueless.</p>

<p>new2irc
type  /ircintro  (by itself on a new line in any window) to see mirc&#8217;s built-in intro, or see a short intro to IRC at http://www.irchelp.org/irchelp/new2irc.html</p>

<p>ops?
An existing op can give you ops, or you can become an op by starting your own new channel: /join #channel-name-that-doesn&#8217;t-exist. Starting a channel is trivial, but running one properly is a very advanced subject.</p>

<p>ping
Pings are small signals used to measure lag, the delay time it takes before others see what you type, try: /ping $N or /ping $C</p>

<p>ping pong
The server PINGs you periodically to see if you&#8217;re still alive, mIRC automatically confirms with a PONG so you don&#8217;t get disconnected. These messages are normal, but you can hide them: alt-o | irc | options.</p>

<p>register
Sorry, you can&#8217;t register nicks or channels on this network (EFnet), it&#8217;s all 1st come 1st served. If those services are really important to you, try other nets like DALnet.</p>

<p>server ops
you&#8217;re describing server ops. Those people had ops before their server split, so when their server rejoined, they were given ops back.</p>

<p>split = ?
Sometimes IRC servers have technical difficulties and temporarily disconnect or &#8220;split&#8221; from each other. People on one side of the split see those on the other side signing off all at once, but they will return when the servers rejoin.</p>

<p>takeover
start a new channel (mode +stin), ban the takeover people, &amp; /invite the regulars over. Or wait a while, they may get bored &amp; leave. Also go invisible (/mode yournick +i) and change nicks temporarily so they can&#8217;t follow you.</p>

<p>voice
+v channel mode (resulting in + before your nick) lets you talk on moderated channels where otherwise only ops can talk. On un-moderated channels, it does nothing at all and may just indicate a non-op regular.</p>

<p>where am i
Welcome to the #IRChelp channel on EFnet, the largest Internet Relay Chat (IRC) network. Do you have an IRC question?</p>

<p>who <em>email</em>
with a hostname (abc.foo.com) or IP (123.4.56.78), try /who <em>.foo.com or /who 123.4.56.</em> NOTE: almost never works unless they are on your channels already, since 75% of people are invisible and won&#8217;t be shown.</p>

<p>XP firewall
To disable the Internet Connection Firewall (ICF) built into Win XP, bring up Properties for your connection (dialup, cable, DSL, etc), click &#8220;Advanced&#8221; tab, uncheck the box to disable it, and reboot. This should fix your DCC send and identd problems.</p>
